An {\em extensor} \nota{in french, it is \q{\textfrench{extenseur}}} for a category \(\cat C\) amounts at having one object \(a\) of \(\cat C\) and for every {\em span}
\[\begin{tikzcd}[row sep=tiny]
& x \ar["f", dl, swap] \ar["g", dr] & \\
a & & y
\end{tikzcd}\]
of \(\cat C\) one morphism \(y \to a\) of \(\cat C\) we denote \(\angled{f, g}\); all this complying with the following rules:
\begin{enumerate}
\item For any consecutive morphisms \begin{tikzcd}[column sep=tiny] x \ar["g", r] & y \ar["h", r] & z \end{tikzcd} we have
\[\angled{\angled{f, g}, h} = \angled{f, hg}.\]
